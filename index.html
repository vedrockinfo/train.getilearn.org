Our Inspiration

Late Dr. Jagdish Gandhi

Late Dr Jagdish Gandhi was one of India's most visionary educationists, the founder of City Montessori School, Lucknow, which he grew from five children in 1959 into the world's largest school with over 64,000 students—recognised repeatedly by the Guinness World Records. A lifelong advocate of world unity and peace, he dedicated more than six decades to building an education model that shaped not just capable students but compassionate world citizens. He convened the International Conferences of Chief Justices of the World, a landmark platform that brought together nearly 1500 judges from 143 countries to champion a safe and just future for the world's children. His work earned global acclaim, including honorary doctorates across continents, the prestigious UNESCO Prize for Peace Education, the Yash Bharti Award, and numerous international honours for quality education and peacebuilding. Guided by the ideals of Mahatma Gandhi and the teachings of the Bahá'í Faith, he lived simply and worked tirelessly, carrying forward his mission with remarkable discipline, humility and purpose until his last days.

Our Inspiration

Dr. Bharti Gandhi

Dr Bharti Gandhi is one of India’s pioneering educationists, a psychologist by training and the force behind the creation and growth of City Montessori School, Lucknow—today the world’s largest school and a global symbol of values-driven education. For more than six decades, she has led with a rare mix of academic depth and moral clarity, shaping early childhood education, establishing India’s first large-scale teacher-training ecosystems, and championing the empowerment of girls and women. Her vision helped CMS win the prestigious UNESCO Prize for Peace Education, and her own contributions have been recognised through honours such as the Devi Award, the Rani Laxmi Bai Bravery Award, the Visionary of Uttar Pradesh Award, and numerous lifetime recognitions for service to children and society. Widely travelled and deeply committed to global harmony, she brought international best practices to India long before globalisation was a buzzword. Above all, she stands for the belief that education is not just about achievement, but about nurturing character, dignity and a sense of world citizenship in every child.

Why ALfA x Hi PATH

In three quick contrasts

Exams vs Life

Old

Education mainly for board exams and jobs.

New

Education for life, soul and citizenship, with exams as one checkpoint - not the destination.

Teaching vs Galvanising

Old

Teacher as instructor or 'coach' who explains and controls.

New

Teacher as galvaniser who sets up powerful routines, then steps back so children take the lead.

Rows vs Pairs

Old

Children sit in rows, work alone, and are compared constantly.

New

Children work in pairs and groups, support each other, and learn to think from another's perspective.
