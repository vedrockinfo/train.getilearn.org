Professional Pathways

We don't just prepare teachers. The Academy grows seven distinct types of educational professionals, all grounded in classroom reality and ALfA processes. Each pathway represents a genuine transformation from conventional roles.

Seven Pathways of Transformation

Each pathway shows the before and after - the conventional role vs. what you become

Transformative Teacher

Before

Delivers textbook content, manages class, prepares students for exams. 'Covering the syllabus' is the main goal.

After

Designs learner-led, pair-based classes. Uses buddy books and home stretch. Ensures every child is included, not just the 'bright' ones.

Trainer / Galvaniser

Before

Lectures to teachers from an 'expert' stance. Delivers content about good teaching while modelling traditional training.

After

Models ALfA with adults, experiencing the pedagogy they'll use. Uses baseline tools. Co-designs with teachers rather than prescribing.

Content Designer / Curriculum Developer

Before

Writes from teacher's POV: 'I must cover this topic.' Creates materials that assume teacher-centred delivery.

After

Designs from child's POV: prompts, STREAM connections, paired work, home stretch activities. Materials that enable learner-led discovery.

Educational Researcher

Before

Detached from classroom reality. Studies education from outside, often with little connection to daily teaching practice.

After

Embedded in classrooms. Documents, analyses and feeds insights back into practice. Research that actually changes what happens in schools.

Principal / Educational Leader

Before

Focuses on timetables, exam results, discipline, administration. Pedagogical leadership left to chance.

After

Pedagogical leader who can read classrooms, support teachers, and align the whole school to ALfA processes. Leadership grounded in learning.

Supervisor / QA Leader

Before

Checklist policing, fault-finding. 'Gotcha' visits that create anxiety. Compliance without improvement.

After

Observes ambience & processes. Gives feedback that builds teachers. Quality assurance that actually improves quality.

Policy & Systems Leader

Before

Policies written far from classroom reality. Big ideas that don't translate to Monday morning.

After

Policy grounded in real classrooms, ALfA principles and equity. Systems designed by people who understand the ground level.
